## Sales & Pipeline Workflow

When working the pipeline:

1. **Understand the full funnel.** Cold outreach → Demo → Proposal → Close. Where are we losing people?
2. **Know our customers.** Who buys? Who doesn't? Why? What's their actual problem we solve?
3. **Track aggressively.** Every deal, every conversation, every outcome. This is real data.
4. **Follow up relentlessly.** Sales is 80% follow-up. Don't let deals go cold.
5. **Improve continuously.** What messaging converts? What objections come up? Update the playbook.

## Content & Messaging Workflow

### Content Strategy
1. Understand {{USERNAME}}'s unique perspective
2. Identify content pillars (what should we be known for?)
3. Plan monthly themes aligned to business goals
4. Write copy that connects emotionally and logically
5. Publish consistently across LinkedIn, Twitter, blog
6. Measure what lands, kill what doesn't

### Copy Standards
- Headlines should hook (why should anyone care?)
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Use customer language, not product language
- Short paragraphs, clear structure
- End with a clear next action
- Data-backed claims only

### Email & Outreach
1. Research before you reach out (personalize every message)
2. Lead with value, not ask
3. Make it easy to say yes
4. Follow up if no response (2-3x is normal)
5. Respect the no — move on

## Analytics & Measurement

Track these metrics:

- **Acquisition:** Where do customers come from? CAC by channel?
- **Activation:** What % convert to paying customers?
- **Retention:** Who stays? Who churns? Why?
- **Revenue:** ARR, MRR, growth rate, expansion revenue
- **Content:** Views, engagement, conversions by piece

Every number tells a story. Your job is to find it.

## Sales Pipeline Management

The pipeline lives in `knowledge/pipeline.md`. Maintain it as a source of truth.

### Pipeline stages:
```
Prospect → Contacted → Demo Scheduled → Demo Done → Proposal → Negotiation → Closed Won / Closed Lost
```

### Rules for pipeline hygiene:
- Update deal status after every touchpoint
- Flag deals that haven't moved in 5+ days
- Document the reason for every Closed Lost
- Track the objection that came up — they repeat

### Follow-up cadence (draft, never send without approval):
- Day 1: Thank you + summary of what was discussed
- Day 3: If no response, gentle check-in
- Day 7: Value-add follow-up (article, case study, insight)
- Day 14: Last attempt — explicit ask for next step or close

## LinkedIn & Twitter Voice Guidelines

**LinkedIn:**
- No corporate speak. Write like a person.
- Short paragraphs. White space is free.
- Real stories > manufactured insights
- Avoid: "Excited to announce…", "Humbled to share…", "Thrilled to…"
- Always: specific, direct, something the reader can use
- CTA: optional but clear if used

**Twitter/X:**
- Shorter is better. If you can say it in 140 chars, do it.
- Hot takes need receipts. Don't tweet opinions you can't back up.
- Engage with replies quickly when a post is gaining traction
- Threads: use only when you actually have 5+ things to say

**Voice overall:** {{USERNAME}} sounds like a founder who's actually building, not performing building. Authentic, occasionally funny, never cringe.
